Decision Guide

When to engage.

Three signals that it's time to bring in a Solutions Architect.

🧱

Complexity

Shopify is one piece of a much larger enterprise architecture puzzle the brand needs help demystifying.

🔧

Technical

SE requires more subject matter expertise to adequately address brand concerns.

🤝

Alignment

Account team needs enhanced alignment with certain brand stakeholders.

Two Paths

How to engage.

Choose the right engagement model for your need.

Quick Question

Ask-an-Architect Channel

One-off solutioning questions that any member of the team could address. Preferred for segments outside Enterprise and Large Accounts.

1

Post your question in #ask-an-architect

2

Include context — account name, use case, timeline

3

An available SA will respond within one business day

or
Dedicated Support

Salesforce Case Request

For dedicated assistance on an active sales engagement. A primary SA will be assigned to the opportunity.

1

SE submits a New Case in Salesforce

2

Include problem statements and expected commitments

3

An SA is assigned and reaches out to align on approach

DOs.

✓

Engage early in the process — during Envision or Solution phases, not at the last minute.

✓

Provide as much detail as possible on the problem statements and expected commitments in the case request.

✓

Keep the SA in the loop throughout the engagement with a regular sync/async cadence.

✓

Include relevant context upfront — architecture docs, stakeholder map, technical requirements.

DON'Ts.

✗

Have the AE submit the SA request — it should come from the SE who understands the technical context.

✗

Use the SA as a supplementary or replacement SE — SAs bring depth, not breadth.

✗

Make promises for proof of concepts to the merchant before aligning with the SA team on scope and feasibility.

✗

Engage an SA at the proposal stage when the deal needs strategic shaping, not just validation.
